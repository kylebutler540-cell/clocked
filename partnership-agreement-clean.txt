PARTNERSHIP AGREEMENT


THIS PARTNERSHIP AGREEMENT (the "Agreement") made and entered into this 20th day of April, 2026,

BETWEEN:

Lukas Achterhof of 5830 Burton St SE, Grand Rapids, MI 49546, USA, and
Tyler Dickinson of 6304 Bechalla Dr SE, Grand Rapids, MI 49546, USA
(individually the "Partner" and collectively the "Partners").


BACKGROUND:

The Partners wish to go into business together and this Agreement sets out the terms that govern their Partnership.

IN CONSIDERATION OF the mutual promises made in this Agreement, the parties agree as follows:


1. Formation

By this Agreement the Partners enter into a general partnership (the "Partnership") in accordance with the laws of the State of Michigan.


2. Name

The firm name of the Partnership will be: Clocked.


3. Purpose

The purpose of the Partnership is to develop, operate, and commercialize a mobile and web-based anonymous workplace review platform known as "Clocked," including all related software, products, services, and business activities associated therewith.


4. Term

The Partnership will begin on April 20th, 2026 and will continue until terminated as provided in this Agreement.


5. Place of Business

The principal office of the Partnership will be located at 5830 Burton St SE, Grand Rapids, MI 49546, USA or such other place as the Partners may agree upon.


6. Ownership and Contributions

Both Partners agree to an equal ownership interest in the Partnership of fifty percent (50%) each. Both Partners agree to contribute equally to the Partnership. Specific dollar amounts and contribution schedules will be documented in a separate written addendum signed by both Partners when applicable.


7. Profit and Loss

All net profits and losses of the Partnership will be divided equally between the Partners - fifty percent (50%) to Lukas Achterhof and fifty percent (50%) to Tyler Dickinson.


8. Management and Decisions

All decisions regarding the management, operation, and control of the Partnership will be decided by unanimous agreement of both Partners. Neither Partner may bind the Partnership in any contract or agreement without the consent of the other Partner.


9. Banking

The funds of the Partnership will be held in accounts designated by both Partners. All withdrawals from Partnership bank accounts require the authorization of both Partners. Partnership funds will not be mixed with personal funds of either Partner.


10. Financial Records

Accurate books of account will be kept for the Partnership and will be available for inspection by either Partner at any time.


11. Fiscal Year

The fiscal year will end on December 31 of each year.


12. Ownership of Partnership Assets

All assets, intellectual property, software, code, brand names, trademarks, and any other property developed or acquired by the Partnership belong to the Partnership equally. Neither Partner may claim sole ownership of any Partnership asset. This includes but is not limited to the Clocked app, its codebase, its brand, and any associated accounts or revenue streams.


13. Duty of Loyalty

Neither Partner will engage in any business that competes with the Partnership without the written consent of the other Partner. A Partner who leaves the Partnership may not carry on a similar competing business for a period of two (2) years after their departure.


14. Admitting a New Partner

A new Partner may only be admitted with the unanimous written consent of both existing Partners.


15. Voluntary Withdrawal

Either Partner may voluntarily withdraw from the Partnership by providing the other Partner with at least six (6) months written notice. The withdrawing Partner's interest will be valued and purchased as described in the Valuation section of this Agreement.


16. Involuntary Withdrawal

A Partner may be involuntarily removed from the Partnership for reasons including but not limited to: death, mental incapacity, criminal conviction, breach of this Agreement, or conduct that damages the reputation or business of the Partnership.


17. Valuation of Interest

If a Partner leaves or is removed, the value of their interest will be determined by a fair market value appraisal conducted by an independent accounting firm agreed upon by both Partners. The remaining Partner has the right of first purchase on the departing Partner's interest.


18. Dissolution

The Partnership may only be dissolved by the unanimous written consent of both Partners. Upon dissolution, all debts will be paid first, and remaining assets will be divided equally between the Partners.


19. Amendments

This Agreement may only be amended by unanimous written consent of both Partners.


20. Governing Law

This Agreement is governed by the laws of the State of Michigan.


21. Entire Agreement

This Agreement represents the entire understanding between the Partners. No verbal agreements or prior understandings override this written Agreement.
